CIRCUS

The Circus album is the second studio album released by Eraserheads in 1994, it has a length of 59:45. This is an iconic album of the band since it is the first album that uses an inverted letter E, a notable trademark of the band. The tracks of the album includes: Bato, Sembreak, Alapaap, Hey Jay, Minsan, Punk Zappa, Insomya, With a smile, Alkohol, Wishing wells, kailan, Magasin, Butterscotch, Sa wakas, Prof. Banlaoi's Transcendental Medication, After Every Six Months, and waiting

ULTRAELECTROMAGNETICPOP

Ultraelectromagneticpop! is the debut studio album of Eraserheads. It was released in July 1993. Most tracks and compositions in this album were written during the college days of the band members. The tracks of the album includes: Easy ka lang, Maling akala, Pare ko, Shake yer head, Ganjazz, Toyang, Ligaya, Tindahan ni aleng Nena, Honky-Toinks Granny, Shirley, Walang-hiyang pare ko, and Combo on the run

STICKER HAPPY

Stickerhappy is the fifth and most controversial studio album of Eraserheads. It was released on September 11, 1997. The album contains eighteen tracks with a 67-minute runtime.

The tracks of the albums includes: Prologue, Futuristic, Kaliwete, Milk and money, Bogchi Hokbu, Maaalalahanin, Balikbayan box, Andalusian Dog, Ha ha ha, Downtown, Kananete, Hard to believe, Everything they say, Spoliarium, Ambi dextrose, Para sa masa, Sticker happy, and Tapsilogue
